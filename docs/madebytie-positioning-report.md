## A note before you start reading

You asked me to study Kree8, Baked, and other studios "delivering what you can," and figure out how to position you as a leader in that space.

Here's the thing I have to say upfront because if I bury it, you'll skim past it: **you are not in the same space as Kree8 and Baked.** They're playing a different game than you. You can borrow tactics from them — and you should, several are excellent — but the core strategy of "be like Kree8 but solo" is not viable for you, and trying to execute it would cost you months and probably your business.

I'll show you why with actual numbers and structural analysis below. Then I'll give you a real positioning that's adjacent to theirs but uniquely yours, and a much more achievable path to a leader position in *that* space.

The TLDR for when you wake up:

1. Kree8 and Baked are **teams of 5-10+ people running productized design subscriptions**. You're one person who can also build full products. Those are different businesses.
2. Their model is brilliant for what they do — but it requires either a team or extreme deliverable scope-tightening that would erase your biggest advantage.
3. Your real positioning is closer to a hybrid: **a solo "fractional product partner" who does the design + build of full products end-to-end**, with productized entry points and a retainer for ongoing work. Not pure subscription. Not pure custom-project. A specific hybrid.
4. Concrete pricing, page structure, copy, and offer recommendations are in section 5.
5. Scope Architect plays a specific strategic role I'll explain in section 4 — it's bigger than just "another portfolio item."

## 1. What Kree8 actually is

### The setup
- **Run by Jay Dwivedi and Satya** (co-founders), with a team of at least 5 named designers visible on their site (Ajibola, Farhan, Hossein, Rehan, Ishan).
- "Designers first. Founders second" — they explicitly call themselves a small team, not a solo studio.
- **"Worked with 130+ brands, helped 50+ founders raise $200M+"** — that's the credibility hook. Their entire pitch leans on founder-investor money raised.
- Target audience: **funded early-stage founders** (pre-seed to Series A) who need to ship product and look fundable.

### The offer
Two paths, very clearly:

**One-time projects:**
- Landing Page: $5,149 (15-20 days)
  - +$2,799 for development (Next.js, Framer, or Webflow)
  - +$800/page for extras
  - +$800/animation
- Branding: separate pricing
- Product Animation (2D/3D): separate
- Mobile App Design: separate

**Retainer:**
- $7,199/month
- 1 active task at a time
- Includes everything: landing pages, logos, mobile apps, web apps, product design, social, animations
- Updates every 48 hours
- No revision limit
- Cancelable

### Why it works
- **Crystal-clear pricing.** No "let's hop on a call to scope it." You know what you'll pay before you ever talk to them.
- **Productized scope = predictable margin.** They've done so many landing pages they know exactly how long one takes. Their cost basis is locked in, so the $5,149 is mostly margin minus team labor.
- **Team structure.** When they hit a wall, they have multiple designers to flex. Solo operators don't have this.
- **Single sales channel: Telegram.** "Send a Message" goes to Jay's personal Telegram. This is meaningful — they've engineered an inbound-only motion. They don't do outbound. The site does the selling.
- **Founder-network referrals.** "50+ founders raised $200M+" is the kind of stat that gets passed around in YC/Cluely/Contra-style founder Discords. They get refer-in traffic from happy founders to their next-startup friends.

### Why it would not work for you, as is

Three structural problems:

**a) Their unit economics depend on team labor specialization.** A $5,149 landing page that takes 15-20 days requires multiple specialists touching it (UI designer, motion, dev). One person doing all of that in 15-20 days is barely break-even at $5K. You'd need to charge ~$10K solo for similar scope to make it work, and at $10K the offer no longer feels like a productized deal — it feels like a normal project.

**b) The retainer model assumes high-volume small deliverables.** $7,199/mo with 48-hour updates and "1 active task" means the average task ships in 2-3 days. That works for branding tweaks, landing pages, social graphics, slide decks. It does **not** work for "full brand + product + development from scratch," which is the work you actually do at the highest level.

**c) No outbound, all inbound.** Kree8's entire growth depends on the founder network already knowing them. You don't have that network. You'd be running a Kree8-style site with no Kree8-style traffic, and the site would convert at 0%.

### What to actually steal from Kree8
- The "One-time OR Retainer, your choice" pricing presentation. Yes. Adopt this.
- The clarity of named deliverables with prices visible.
- The "Add Development +$2,799" toggle on the landing page card. Brilliant. Lets the buyer self-select the level of engagement.
- Time-to-delivery shown on every offer (15-20 days). Reduces "how long will this take" anxiety.
- "Updates every 48 hours" as a service-level commitment. Sets expectation, builds trust.
- The two co-founders showing their faces with X profiles. Builds personal trust.
- The named team grid. Even though you're solo, you can show your face prominently the way Jay and Satya do — that personal stake matters.

## 2. What Baked Design actually is

### The setup
- Founded by Nick Buzz and Ali Suzu, July 2023.
- Went from $4K MRR to $1M ARR in 3 months (their own claim). Now estimated ~$100K+ MRR.
- **Team of senior designers with backgrounds at Netflix, Meta, BMW.**
- Pitched as a "design subscription for startup founders."

### The offer
- **Pure subscription model.** No one-time project pricing visible.
- Tiered subscription (exact current pricing they don't publish openly — they shifted to "book a call" gating, which is itself a signal that pricing has gotten higher / more bespoke as they've scaled).
- Reports suggest tiers in the $5K-$12K/month range based on team scale.
- Pause/cancel anytime.
- Senior designer-led, but team-fulfilled.
- 24-hour first response, async-first.

### Why it works
- **Brand of brands.** "Made by makers for makers" + Netflix/Meta pedigree is catnip for funded founders.
- **They built an audience first.** Nick Buzz and Ali Suzu were already running "Indie Design Kit" — a popular ProductHunt design resource — before launching Baked. They had a 50K+ audience to launch into. That's how they hit $1M ARR in 3 months. It wasn't the model; it was the audience.
- **Senior-only positioning.** They explicitly do NOT take junior designers. This justifies the premium.
- **Subscription only.** Forces commitment, eliminates scoping overhead, locks in MRR.

### Why it would not work for you, as is

Same three problems as Kree8 plus one more:

**d) The audience-first launch is irreplicable post-hoc.** You can't "launch with an audience" if you don't have one yet. You'd need to spend 6-12 months building the audience first (which I'll talk about in section 6).

### What to steal from Baked
- The senior-only positioning, repackaged as "one craftsman who's senior at all of it." That's actually more rare than Baked's senior team.
- The async-first commitment. State it explicitly.
- The 24-hour first response SLA.
- The "pause anytime" psychological safety on the retainer. Removes the biggest objection.

## 3. The pattern across all of them

I looked at Kree8, Baked, Designjoy, Studiolit, Design Retainer Studio, DGecko, and a handful of others. The pattern across the successful ones:

| Element | Common Approach | Your Current State |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing on homepage | Specific numbers visible | $10K+ vague starting point |
| Productized deliverables | Named, scoped, priced | Custom every time |
| Single primary CTA | "Book a call" repeated 5+ times | "Start a project" with variations |
| Time-to-deliver shown | Always visible | Not stated |
| One-time + Retainer offered | Both, choose | Both, but retainer feels secondary |
| Founder-led personal brand | Founder face, X, story | Logo-led, not face-led |
| Inbound from audience | Twitter, ProductHunt, IndieHackers | None yet |
| Niche/vertical focus | Startups + funded founders | "Premium digital products" (broad) |
| Trust signals | Specific raised $$$ / company names | Logos shown but not contextualized |

**The throughline:** these businesses have **removed friction at every step of the buyer's journey.** Specific pricing. Specific timelines. Specific deliverables. Specific founder personality. Specific niche audience. The buyer never has to wonder.

Your site is good but it has too many "let's discuss" moments. Each one is a place where a buyer drops off.

This is the move Kree8 and Baked can't make. They sell design services. You sell **someone who understands design AND product AND business model AND can ship the whole thing.** That positioning is rare and defensible.

## 5. Recommended positioning, offer, and pricing for madebytie

### Positioning statement

**"One craftsman. End-to-end. The full brand-to-product partner for founders who'd rather work with a senior solo than coordinate a team."**

The market gap you fill: founders who've been burned by agencies (too many cooks, expensive, slow) and freelancers (too narrow, can't ship product), and who want one senior person who can do it all. This is your Kree8/Baked-adjacent niche, but it's genuinely yours.

### The offer structure

**Three options. Crystal clear. On the homepage with prices.**

#### Option 1: Foundation Build (one-time)
**$15,000 — $35,000** (range stated openly on site)
- Full brand + landing page + product design + initial development
- 4-6 weeks
- Updates every 48 hours
- One craftsman, end-to-end
- For founders launching something new

(Note: $15K floor, not your old $10K. Reason: floor of $10K is widely undervaluing you, and undercharging on this tier hurts you on all sides — buyers question quality, and you burn out. $15K is still well below agency rates for equivalent scope.)

#### Option 2: Productized Sprints (one-time)
**Smaller, named, scoped deliverables** — Kree8-style.

- Landing Page Sprint: $5,500 (design only, 10-14 days)
  - +$3,500 for development
- Brand Sprint: $4,500 (logo + identity system + guidelines, 10-14 days)
- Pitch Deck Sprint: $3,500 (10 days)
- Product Design Sprint: $7,500 (UI/UX for one product surface, 2-3 weeks)

These are the "I need this one specific thing, fast" options that capture buyers who aren't ready for Foundation.

#### Option 3: Partner Retainer (monthly)
**$7,500/month**
- Available after Foundation OR as standalone
- One active task at a time
- Updates every 48 hours
- Mix and match: design, dev, brand, ongoing iteration
- 24-hour first response
- Pause or cancel anytime
- For founders who need a senior partner in motion

This is your Brett-from-Designjoy/Kree8-retainer equivalent. The MRR engine.

### Why this structure works for you specifically

- **Foundation** is your moonshot revenue. One Foundation = a month's burn. You only need 1-2 per quarter to be stable.
- **Sprints** are your accessibility tier. Founders who can't afford $15K can start with a $4,500 brand sprint and graduate to Foundation or Retainer.
- **Retainer** is your recurring revenue base. After 6 retainer clients, you're at $45K MRR with predictable income.
- **Solo-feasible.** All three can be delivered by one person (you) at the volume the market will support in your first year.
- **Doesn't try to be Kree8.** You can't out-team Kree8. But you can be the senior-solo option for buyers who specifically want that.

## 8. The harder truth about positioning yourself "as a leader"

You said you want to be "a leader in this space" and "competitive with these guys."

I want to be honest with you. **Kree8 and Baked are not leaders because their websites are well-positioned.** They're leaders because they have:

1. Audiences (Nick Buzz had 50K+ followers before launching Baked)
2. Network (Jay and Satya are deep in the YC/Cluely/Contra founder world)
3. Time (Baked is 2.5 years old, Kree8 several years)
4. Public proof (raised money for clients, named startups using them)

You have, today: a great site, real skills, real portfolio, zero audience, near-zero current network in the founder/startup world, and need revenue this quarter.

**Becoming a leader in this space takes 12-24 months minimum.** That's the audience-build time. There's no shortcut. Kree8 and Baked didn't shortcut it. They earned it.

What you can do in the next 90 days:
- Make your site as conversion-optimized as theirs (this report)
- Start the public build motion (LinkedIn/X daily)
- Send the warm outreach motion we built (Bitmain execs, TJ, etc.)
- Close 2-4 deals from existing network + cold founder outreach
- Stabilize income at $10-25K/month

What you can do in the next 12 months:
- Build the audience through consistent build-in-public content
- Have 50+ shipped projects to point to
- Be the senior-solo recommended name in 3-5 founder communities
- Hit consistent $25-50K/month

What you can do in 24 months:
- Be a recognized name in this space, on Kree8/Baked-adjacent positioning

The site changes in this report give you a much better foundation. But the leadership position comes from showing up publicly every day for 12-24 months, not from one weekend of site polish.
